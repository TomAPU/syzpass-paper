\section{Conclusion}
In this paper, we identify the overlooked problem of side effects in Linux kernel exploitation. We propose avoidance and neutralization as two strategies for reasoning about these effects and implement them in SyzPass, an automated framework for exploit primitive analysis and side-effect guidance. In a primitive-aware evaluation over 97 analyzed cases, SyzPass reports 291 candidates under the pointer-materialization model and 15 candidates under the portable object-matching model, including 14 from 10 bugs whose candidacy depends on side-effect neutralization. Finally, we develop three working kernel exploits following SyzPass guidance, covering object matching, path guidance, and constraint guidance.
