\section{Conclusion}
In this paper, we identify the overlooked problem of side effects in Linux kernel exploitation. We propose avoidance and neutralization as two strategies for reasoning about these effects and implement them in SyzPass, an automated framework for exploit primitive analysis and side-effect resolution. In a strict primitive-aware evaluation over 97 complete cases, SyzPass identifies 14 primitive instances from 10 bugs whose usability depends on side-effect neutralization. Finally, we develop three kernel exploits following the guidance of SyzPass, demonstrating its utility in exploit construction.
