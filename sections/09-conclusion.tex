\section{Conclusion}
In this paper, we identify the overlooked problem of side effects in Linux kernel exploitation. We propose avoidance and neutralization as two strategies for reasoning about these effects and implement them in SyzPass, an automated framework for exploit primitive analysis and side-effect guidance. In a strict primitive-aware evaluation over 97 complete cases, SyzPass reports 15 object-matching-only candidate instances, including 14 from 10 bugs whose candidacy depends on side-effect neutralization; pointer materialization adds 276 pointer-related candidates under the stronger capability model. Finally, we develop three expert-assisted kernel exploits following SyzPass guidance, with one validating a counted object-matching neutralization candidate and two validating path/constraint guidance.
